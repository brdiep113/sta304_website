Part I. Introduction

The divide between English and French Canadians has historically been the source of much social tension in this country. Franco-Canadians have historically found themselves in opposition to and oppressed by the English majority. This disparity can bee seen in historical records. Part II. Data and Analysis

The data used in this report was gathered from the web 2019 Canadian Election Study. The CES consists of a survey given to a large sample of Canadians across the country and asked respondents information regarding many cultural and socioeconomic factors. Specifically, we examine income distribution, educational attainment, and employment status as key indicators that allow us to measure the relative success of French and English Canadians as a group.

Limitations and Next Steps

For the sake of transparency, a few limitations may have affected the results and conclusions made in this study. In particular, we did not take into account the province of residence of respondents. This is particularly an issue as the distribution of English and French speakers in Canada remains extremely stratified between provinces. For example, the majority of Francophones are found in Quebec and New Brunswick.[cite] Quebec is also unique in that it serves as an outlier since it is the only province with a Francophone majority.[cite] Therefore, the experience of being an English Canadian is likely to be much more different in Quebec than in other English majority provinces.

We also fail to take into account bilingual speakers of both English and French. This also poses an additional challenge as native francophones are much more likely to be bilingual than their allophone counterparts.[cite] Finally, our study omits the growing number of immigrants who learn English and/or French later in life, as well as indigenous Canadians who may have extremely different socioeconomic conditions than established English or French communities.

Future work may examine in depth socioeconomic conditions of bilingual speakers as compared to monolingual speakers. Particular care should be placed upon bilingual French-English speakers compared to monolingual French speakers.
